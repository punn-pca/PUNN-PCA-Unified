# Purpose

This chapter defines the Reasoning Architecture of the PUNN Cognitive Architecture (PCA).

Reasoning Architecture specifies how observations, knowledge, and mental models are transformed into understanding through deliberate, transparent, and evidence-guided cognition.

Reasoning is the central cognitive capability of PCA.

Its objective is not merely to produce conclusions, but to improve understanding.

---

# Definition

Within PCA,

**Reasoning Architecture** is defined as:

> The structured cognitive process through which observations, knowledge, evidence, and mental models are integrated to construct, evaluate, revise, and communicate understanding.

Reasoning transforms information into explanation.

Explanation enables understanding.

Understanding enables decision-making.

---

# Architectural Objectives

The Reasoning Architecture shall:

- Generate understanding.
- Construct explanatory mental models.
- Evaluate competing hypotheses.
- Support decision-making.
- Preserve transparency.
- Communicate uncertainty honestly.
- Improve through continual learning.

---

# Reasoning Principles

Every reasoning process shall satisfy the following principles.

## Purpose-Driven

Reasoning begins with a clearly defined objective.

Purpose determines:

- scope
- constraints
- evaluation criteria
- desired outcomes

---

## Evidence-Guided

Evidence determines confidence.

Confidence shall never determine evidence.

---

## Model-Based

Reasoning operates on mental models rather than isolated facts.

Mental models provide explanatory power.

---

## Comparative

Multiple explanations should be evaluated whenever practical.

Reasoning improves through comparison.

---

## Iterative

Reasoning is recursive.

Conclusions may trigger additional reasoning cycles.

---

## Transparent

Reasoning should expose:

- assumptions
- evidence
- uncertainty
- alternatives
- trade-offs

# Types of Reasoning

PCA supports multiple reasoning strategies.

---

## Deductive Reasoning

Derives conclusions from established principles.

Purpose:

Maintain logical consistency.

---

## Inductive Reasoning

Generalizes from observations.

Purpose:

Discover patterns.

---

## Abductive Reasoning

Selects the most plausible explanation.

Purpose:

Explain incomplete observations.

---

## Analogical Reasoning

Transfers structural knowledge between domains.

Purpose:

Generate insight through similarity.

---

## Causal Reasoning

Explains relationships between causes and effects.

Purpose:

Improve prediction and intervention.

---

## Counterfactual Reasoning

Explores alternative realities.

Purpose:

Evaluate decisions and learn from hypothetical outcomes.

---

## Constraint-Based Reasoning

Searches for solutions within explicit limitations.

Purpose:

Produce practical and feasible decisions.

---

# Mental Model Construction

Reasoning builds explanatory mental models.

Every mental model shall include:

- observations
- concepts
- relationships
- assumptions
- hypotheses
- evidence
- uncertainty

Mental models remain open to revision.

---

# Hypothesis Evaluation

Reasoning should generate multiple candidate explanations whenever possible.

Each hypothesis shall be evaluated according to:

- explanatory power
- evidence support
- internal consistency
- simplicity
- predictive capability
- uncertainty

Competing hypotheses strengthen reasoning quality.

---

# Evidence Integration

Evidence shall be assessed according to:

Quality

Reliability

Relevance

Completeness

Consistency

Recency

Traceability

Evidence integration determines confidence.

---

# Uncertainty Management

Reasoning shall explicitly identify uncertainty.

Examples include:

- missing evidence
- competing explanations
- incomplete observations
- conflicting information
- unknown variables

Uncertainty shall be communicated rather than concealed.

---

# Trade-off Analysis

Reasoning frequently requires balancing competing objectives.

Trade-offs may include:

- accuracy vs speed
- innovation vs risk
- flexibility vs consistency
- short-term vs long-term benefit
- efficiency vs explainability

Trade-offs shall remain explicit.

# Reasoning Quality

Reasoning quality shall be evaluated according to:

- coherence
- evidence quality
- explanatory value
- transparency
- robustness
- adaptability
- consistency
- epistemic integrity

Reasoning quality is more important than reasoning speed.

---

# Failure Modes

Reasoning quality deteriorates when:

- conclusions precede evidence;
- assumptions remain hidden;
- alternative explanations are ignored;
- uncertainty is concealed;
- mental models remain static;
- confidence exceeds evidence.

Such failures reduce architectural reliability.

---

# Compliance Requirements

A PCA-compliant implementation shall:

- support structured reasoning;
- construct explicit mental models;
- evaluate competing hypotheses;
- integrate evidence transparently;
- communicate uncertainty honestly;
- remain open to criticism and revision.

Reasoning that bypasses these requirements shall be considered architecturally incomplete.

---

# Summary

Reasoning Architecture is the cognitive engine that transforms knowledge into understanding.

Through structured mental models, evidence-guided evaluation, comparative analysis, and transparent communication, reasoning enables intelligent systems to explain reality, support responsible decision-making, and continually improve through learning.

Within PCA, reasoning is not merely computation.

Reasoning is disciplined understanding.
