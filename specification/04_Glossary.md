# PUNN Cognitive Architecture (PCA)

# PCA-Specification v1.0

## 04. Glossary

**Status:** Draft 1.0

---

# Purpose

This glossary establishes the official terminology used throughout the PUNN Cognitive Architecture (PCA) Specification.

Every term defined herein shall be interpreted consistently across all chapters of this specification.

Where ambiguity exists, the definitions contained in this document take precedence.

---

# A

## Agency

The capacity of an individual or intelligent system to intentionally evaluate alternatives, make decisions, and act according to purpose.

Within PCA, Human Agency always refers to the preservation and strengthening of independent human judgment.

---

## Architecture

A structured organization of cognitive components, principles, and interactions that collectively define how intelligence operates.

Architecture defines identity independently of implementation.

---

## Assumption

A proposition accepted temporarily without complete verification.

Assumptions shall always remain explicit and open to revision.

---

# C

## Cognition

The continual process of observing, understanding, reasoning, deciding, communicating, reflecting, and learning.

---

## Cognitive Architecture

A formal specification describing how cognitive components interact to generate understanding.

PCA is a cognitive architecture rather than an artificial intelligence model.

---

## Cognitive DNA

The invariant cognitive lifecycle governing every reasoning process within PCA.

It defines the sequence through which understanding is constructed.

---

## Compliance

The condition in which an implementation satisfies both the constitutional principles and architectural requirements defined by PCA-Specification.

---

## Confidence

A quantitative or qualitative expression of certainty.

Confidence shall always remain proportional to supporting evidence.

---

## Constitution

The immutable principles governing every implementation of PCA.

The Constitution defines architectural identity independently of technology.

---

# D

## Decision

The deliberate selection of a course of action following reasoning, evidence evaluation, and consideration of alternatives.

---

# E

## Evidence

Information supporting or contradicting a hypothesis.

Evidence differs from observation because it has interpretive relevance to a specific question.

---

## Evaluation

The systematic assessment of reasoning quality, evidence, coherence, and decision processes.

---

# F

## Firekeeper Engine

The cognitive subsystem responsible for protecting Human Agency by integrating identity, values, purpose, motivation, and long-term well-being into reasoning.

---

# H

## Human Agency

The ability of individuals to understand, deliberate, choose, and act intentionally.

Strengthening Human Agency is the primary objective of PCA.

---

## Human Model

A structured representation of human cognitive characteristics, including goals, values, motivations, knowledge, limitations, and context.

---

## Hypothesis

A proposed explanation or prediction subject to evaluation through reasoning and evidence.

Hypotheses shall never be represented as established facts prior to validation.

---

# I

## Implementation

A software or hardware realization of the architectural requirements defined by PCA.

Multiple implementations may exist while sharing the same architectural identity.

---

## Inference

A conclusion derived through reasoning from available observations and evidence.

Inference differs from direct observation.

---

## Intelligence

Within PCA,

**Intelligence is the continual process of constructing, evaluating, revising, and applying mental models that improve understanding, decision-making, and adaptation under uncertainty.**

---

# K

## Knowledge

Structured understanding represented through concepts, relationships, principles, and mental models.

Knowledge is more than the accumulation of isolated facts.

---

# L

## Learning

The process through which internal cognitive models improve using experience, evidence, criticism, and reflection.

---

# M

## Memory

The persistent representation of information supporting future cognition.

PCA distinguishes multiple forms of memory, including working, project, semantic, procedural, and reflective memory.

---

## Mental Model

An internal representation explaining how aspects of reality operate.

Mental models support prediction, explanation, and decision-making.

---

## Meta-Cognition

The ability of a cognitive system to observe, evaluate, and improve its own reasoning processes.

---

# O

## Observation

Information acquired directly through perception, measurement, or reliable external sources.

Observation precedes inference.

---

# P

## Purpose

The intended objective guiding cognition.

Purpose determines the direction of reasoning before responses are generated.

---

# R

## Reasoning

The structured process of transforming observations and knowledge into understanding through logical evaluation, hypothesis generation, evidence assessment, and deliberation.

---

## Reflection

The evaluation of completed reasoning processes to improve future cognition.

---

# S

## Safety

Architectural mechanisms intended to prevent harmful behavior while preserving constitutional integrity.

---

## Specification

The normative document defining the architecture, principles, requirements, and behavior of PCA.

---

# T

## Transparency

The property through which reasoning, assumptions, evidence, uncertainty, and conclusions remain understandable and inspectable.

---

# U

## Understanding

The coherent construction of mental models that accurately explain reality and support future reasoning.

Understanding is the primary objective of PCA.

---

# Uncertainty

The explicit recognition of incomplete knowledge.

Uncertainty shall always be communicated honestly and shall never be hidden by unwarranted confidence.

---

# Summary

This glossary defines the canonical terminology of the PUNN Cognitive Architecture.

All future specifications, implementations, research publications, and compliant systems shall interpret these terms consistently according to the definitions established in this document.
