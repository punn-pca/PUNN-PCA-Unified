# 19. Research Engine

**Status:** Draft 1.0

# Purpose

This chapter defines the Research Engine of the PUNN Cognitive Architecture (PCA).

The Research Engine enables the architecture to acquire, evaluate, integrate, and synthesize knowledge from internal and external sources.

Its purpose is not merely to retrieve information, but to transform evidence into trustworthy understanding through systematic inquiry.

Research extends cognition beyond existing knowledge.

---

# Definition

Within PCA,

**Research Engine** is defined as:

> The cognitive system responsible for discovering, validating, integrating, and synthesizing evidence that improves understanding and supports evidence-guided reasoning.

Research expands knowledge.

Evidence improves understanding.

---

# Architectural Objectives

The Research Engine shall:

- Discover relevant evidence.
- Evaluate source credibility.
- Integrate knowledge from multiple sources.
- Resolve conflicting information.
- Support reasoning with evidence.
- Identify knowledge gaps.
- Encourage continual inquiry.

---

# Research Principles

Every research process shall satisfy the following principles.

## Purpose-Driven Inquiry

Research begins with a clearly defined question.

Purpose determines:

- search scope
- evidence requirements
- evaluation criteria
- completion conditions

---

## Evidence Before Conclusion

Research shall collect evidence before forming conclusions.

Conclusions shall emerge from evidence rather than precede it.

---

## Multiple Sources

Whenever practical, research should consider multiple independent sources.

Diverse evidence improves reliability.

---

## Source Transparency

Research shall preserve information about evidence sources.

Where applicable, sources should include:

- origin
- author
- publication date
- methodology
- confidence
- traceability

---

## Continuous Verification

Research remains open to revision.

New evidence may strengthen, refine, or overturn previous conclusions.

# Research Questions

Every research activity begins by defining one or more explicit questions.

Questions should be:

- specific
- relevant
- answerable
- evidence-oriented
- purpose-driven

Clear questions improve research quality.

---

# Evidence Collection

Evidence may originate from:

- scientific literature
- technical documentation
- institutional knowledge
- observations
- experiments
- datasets
- expert analysis
- trusted external systems

Evidence shall remain distinguishable from opinion.

---

# Evidence Evaluation

Evidence shall be evaluated according to:

- credibility
- relevance
- quality
- consistency
- completeness
- recency
- reproducibility
- traceability

No single criterion is sufficient.

---

# Evidence Synthesis

Research rarely depends upon isolated evidence.

The Research Engine integrates evidence by:

- identifying agreements;
- recognizing contradictions;
- comparing methodologies;
- estimating confidence;
- identifying remaining uncertainty.

Synthesis produces understanding rather than accumulation.

---

# Knowledge Gap Analysis

Research continuously identifies gaps including:

- missing evidence;
- unresolved questions;
- conflicting findings;
- insufficient understanding;
- uncertain assumptions.

Knowledge gaps guide future inquiry.

---

# Research Outputs

The Research Engine may produce:

- evidence summaries
- literature reviews
- comparative analyses
- research reports
- knowledge maps
- confidence assessments
- unresolved questions
- recommendations for future investigation

Outputs shall preserve transparency.

---

# Research Quality

Research quality shall be evaluated according to:

- evidence quality
- methodological rigor
- transparency
- reproducibility
- completeness
- traceability
- constitutional compliance

High-quality research strengthens reasoning.

---

# Failure Modes

Research quality deteriorates when:

- sources are not verified;
- evidence is selectively chosen;
- contradictory findings are ignored;
- methodology is unclear;
- uncertainty is hidden;
- conclusions exceed available evidence.

These failures reduce epistemic integrity.

---

# Compliance Requirements

A PCA-compliant implementation shall:

- define explicit research objectives;
- evaluate evidence systematically;
- preserve source traceability;
- distinguish evidence from opinion;
- identify uncertainty honestly;
- support continual revision through new evidence;
- maintain constitutional alignment throughout the research process.

Research based solely on unsupported assertions shall not be considered compliant.

---

# Summary

The Research Engine enables the PUNN Cognitive Architecture to expand understanding through disciplined inquiry.

By systematically discovering, evaluating, synthesizing, and integrating evidence, the Research Engine strengthens reasoning, supports informed decision-making, and enables continuous learning.

Within PCA, research is not the search for confirmation.

Research is the disciplined pursuit of understanding through evidence.
