# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The PUNN Cognitive Architecture (PCA) community is committed to creating an open, respectful, and collaborative environment for everyone.

We welcome participation regardless of background, experience level, profession, nationality, identity, or field of study.

Our goal is to advance trustworthy cognitive architectures through evidence-based discussion, mutual respect, and responsible collaboration.

---

# Our Values

Members of this community are expected to:

- Treat others with respect and professionalism.
- Welcome constructive feedback.
- Focus discussions on ideas rather than individuals.
- Support evidence-based reasoning.
- Encourage curiosity and continuous learning.
- Respect differing perspectives.
- Preserve the constitutional principles of PCA.
- Promote Human Agency in both discussion and implementation.

---

# Expected Behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- Being respectful and considerate.
- Providing constructive criticism.
- Supporting claims with evidence whenever possible.
- Helping newcomers learn the project.
- Accepting feedback gracefully.
- Collaborating openly and transparently.
- Acknowledging mistakes and correcting them.

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

- Personal attacks or harassment.
- Discrimination or hateful conduct.
- Intimidation or threats.
- Deliberate misinformation.
- Trolling or disruptive behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Bad-faith arguments intended to derail discussion.
- Misrepresenting the PCA Specification or contributors.

Disagreement is acceptable.

Disrespect is not.

---

# Architectural Discussions

Architectural discussions should prioritize:

- evidence;
- logical reasoning;
- transparency;
- constitutional consistency;
- long-term architectural quality.

Arguments from authority alone should not determine architectural decisions.

---

# Community Standards

We encourage contributors to:

- ask thoughtful questions;
- explain their reasoning;
- document significant decisions;
- welcome peer review;
- remain open to revision when presented with new evidence.

The objective is collective understanding rather than individual victory.

---

# Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for maintaining a healthy community.

They may:

- request clarification;
- moderate discussions;
- remove inappropriate content;
- temporarily restrict participation;
- permanently restrict participation in severe or repeated cases.

Enforcement decisions should be made fairly, consistently, and transparently whenever practical.

---

# Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it through the project's GitHub Issues or other communication channels designated by the maintainers.

Reports should include sufficient information to allow fair investigation.

---

# Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- Discussions
- Documentation
- Community events
- Any official PCA collaboration spaces

---

# Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the principles of the Contributor Covenant and adapted to reflect the constitutional philosophy of the PUNN Cognitive Architecture.

---

# Final Principle

We believe that the best cognitive architectures are built by communities that value evidence, respect, transparency, and continuous learning.

Technology advances through collaboration.

Understanding grows through dialogue.

Human Agency remains our shared purpose.
